    VR game: Classical Gardens of Suzhou (2022.8-Now)

    Finalist in The 18 th China International Cartoon & Animation Festival Game Competition (Top 5%)

    Classical Gardens of Suzhou

    Project Description:

    The Classical Gardens of Suzhou (Chinese:苏州园林) are a group of gardens in the city of Suzhou, in Jiangsu, China, which have been added to the UNESCO World Heritage List.The Classical Chinese garden design, which seeks to recreate natural landscapes in miniature, is nowhere better illustrated than in the nine gardens in the historic city of Suzhou. They are generally acknowledged to be masterpieces of the genre. Dating from the 11th-19th century, the gardens reflect the profound metaphysical importance of natural beauty in Chinese culture in their meticulous design.

    The game is based on UE4 and virtual headset devices. The main gameplay of this work is that the player has to modify the style of buildings, add or delete buildings in the scene according to the task instructions of the drawing within a specified time. The player has to constantly explore the scenes and observe the building modification categories. At the same time, it takes resources and workers to create buildings, so players need to plan wisely, explore the scene,s collect resources, dispatch workers and trade with merchants within a certain period of time to finally complete the garden scene as instructed in the drawing. The game will eventually be rated according to the player’s level of completion, and after reaching a certain level, you can unlock the roaming mode of the current level and talk to the characters in the garden to start the main plot.

    The levels are based on real gardens and their backstories, relying on the layout of existing gardens, rationalised based on humanistic connotations, and popularised to players. The first level, for example, is adapted from the “Retreat Garden” built by Ren Lansheng in the Qing Dynasty. The environment is examined from the actual layout and incorporates a realistic construction backstory. After completing the construction, players will be given a lesson on the real human background story from which it was taken.

    Graduation Project: 3D Chinese Ink Style Rendering Method and The Scene Procedural Generation （2022.3）

    Supervisor: Dr. Chen Wang

    Unity3D Real-time Rendering Display

    3D Chinese Ink Style Rendering Method and The Scene Procedural Generation

    Project Description:

    • Analysed previous NPR rendering methods, summarized the material and technical characteristics of Chinese ink paintings, improving the rendering method for wider applicability with GPU pipelines
    • Ink style rendering: Employed transparency blending and UV offset algorithms to achieve contour stroke simulation; utilized double passes and Z-writing method to achieve internal shader; provided multiple interfaces for parametric adjustments, improving frame rate by 15% and user visual satisfaction by 12.4%
    • Procedural generation: analysed mountain painting colouring laws, proposed ink style terrain generation and texture using Perlin noise and fractal Brownian motion, designed a river generation tool with collision detection and Catmull-Rom
    • Scored 91.7, awarded Outstanding Bachelor’s Thesis, HIT (Top 2%, only award in the department)

    “Sacred Tree” Netease 2020 Game Design Competition (Finalist 2020.11)

    Sacred Tree

    Finalist in 2020 NetEase MINI-GAME College Challenge (Top 10%) prize of ￥2,000

    Design Description:
    Sacred Tree is a scrolling game based on the mobile platform, players control the direction of plant growth and avoid obstacles by sliding the screen

    In ancient times, the plague was rampant, the wilderness was barren and every household cried. To protect the earth, the guardian bird Qinggong. He was defeated and fell into the underworld, where he was transformed into a sacred tree seed, containing clear energy. If it grows, it will restore peace to the world.
    Players take on the role of a growing tree, change the direction of the tree’s growth by touching the controls to avoid the ghosts and gods and break through the siege to enter the human world. The tree, with its fearless spirit of upward mobility, speaks of restoration. After the pestilence, the earth is again peaceful and tranquil.

    Falling Flowers (Seven-day short development competition)

    Falling Flowers

    Falling Flowers is a mobile “Parkour Adventure Collection” game. In this game, the player initially controls the movement of the “falling flowers” by tapping the screen in succession, allowing the “falling flowers” to dodge obstacles and float further. When the flower reaches 0 life, the flower will enter the ground and turn into “spring mud”. Alternatively, players can actively choose to “turn into the mud” and enter the underground “mysterious world” to complete a new collection quest. The underground section is a challenging level of collecting and is more varied and interesting. The “flower” and “mud” symbolise the different stages of life, and the process of transformation is a natural cycle that inspires us to look at the highs and lows of life, to take our experiences with us, and to always carry our dreams forward.

    Design Description:

    The game was inspired by the poem “Miscellanies of the Year 1839（5）” by Gong Zizhen- “The fallen flowers are not the heartless ones: Turned to mud, they’ll take better care of th’ new“, which says that everything changes and beautiful flowers can easily drift away with the wind, and after experiencing the beauty along the way, they will eventually lose their glory, and fall to the ground and become “spring mud”, disappearing into the earth. But this is not the end of life, “mud” in the earth’s nourishment, will gradually enrich itself, the thick and thin, its life carries the road ahead, and will eventually nourish the new branches of flowers, in the next spring on the wind-up, the beauty and fragrance will be transmitted to more distant places …..
